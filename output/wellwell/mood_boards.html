References

  • Hims & Hers dark mode brand campaign pages — specifically the way they use a near-black background for hero brand statements before transitioning to white for product/service detail sections
  • Glossier's limited-edition campaign microsites — dark editorial hero, single strong typographic statement, then immediate transition to accessible shopping content below
  • Nue Co. wellness brand — the way they pair a dark moody hero with clean white service/ingredient sections to create a luxury-accessible tension that feels premium without feeling exclusive

Strengths

  • ✓ Highest market differentiation of all four options — WellWell would be instantly recognizable and visually unlike any competitor in the telehealth space
  • ✓ The 'Virtual. Visible. VIP.' tagline was made for this dark hero treatment — the word 'VIP' highlighted in peony against the dark background is a genuinely memorable brand moment
  • ✓ Exceptional visual impact for paid advertising landing pages — dark, high-contrast hero pages have strong performance data in beauty and wellness ad campaigns

Watch Outs

  • ⚠ Conversion Optimization: 8/10 — The dramatic hero creates high stopping power and brand recall; the vivid teal CTA button on the dark background has exceptional contrast and click-through draw; slightly lower than Options A/B because the drama of the dark hero can delay intent-to-book behavior
  • ⚠ Accessibility: 7/10 — White text on dark teal-charcoal background passes WCAG AA for large text but requires checking at all text sizes; the secondary text color (#A8BFBD on #1E2E2C) is the one combination that needs contrast ratio validation before approval — this is the highest accessibility maintenance burden of the four options

Best For

The 30–50 professional woman who has disposable income, values aesthetics as a signal of quality, and is slightly bored by the sameness of every wellness brand
